HMW: Vendor Selection Flow

"How might we help ops pick the right vendor for each job — considering both rate and reliability — without slowing down order creation?"
Current state: Ops picks a vendor from a flat dropdown for each job. No rate visibility — they don't know the cost until the invoice arrives. Vendor selection is based on habit and tribal knowledge ("we always use HaleSun for TikTok FM").

With rate management: We now have rate cards per vendor/service/location. The question becomes: where do we surface this information? Options range from minimal (show rate after selection) to full (auto-select the cheapest vendor). The trade-off is speed vs. informed decision-making.

Reliability data: The system already tracks job completions and rejections per vendor. We can compute a simple "completion rate" and "rejections in last 30 days" metric to help ops assess reliability alongside cost.

Option A: Rate Shown After Selection

Minimal change
How it works: The current create form stays the same — ops picks vendor and service from dropdowns, picks location. After all three are selected, the rate auto-appears as a badge next to the job row. No comparison, no suggestion — just visibility into what this particular vendor charges. If no rate is on file, a "No rate" amber badge appears instead.
Pros
- Minimal change to current workflow
- Non-intrusive — rate appears passively
- No extra clicks for power users
- Works even with incomplete rate data
Cons
- Can't compare vendors without switching back and forth
- Ops might not realize a cheaper option exists
- No reliability data visible
- Doesn't encourage cost-optimal decisions

Option B: Vendor Comparison Popover

Recommended
How it works: After ops selects a service and location (but before selecting a vendor), a small "Compare vendors" link appears. Clicking it opens a popover showing all vendors that serve this service at this location, with their rate, unit type, completion rate, and recent rejections. Ops picks a vendor from the comparison table. The popover replaces the vendor dropdown for that job. Power users who already know their vendor can just use the dropdown and ignore the link.
Pros
- Full comparison without leaving the form
- Shows both cost and reliability side by side
- Informed decision-making for new ops
- Power users can ignore it — optional, not forced
Cons
- Extra click for users who want to compare
- Could slow down ops who just want to pick and move on
- Requires rate data for multiple vendors to be useful
- UI complexity: popover positioning, responsive behavior

Option C: Smart Default + Override

Ambitious / future
How it works: The system auto-selects the cheapest available vendor for each job based on rate cards. When ops creates an order and assigns a service + location, the vendor field auto-fills with the cheapest option. It shows "Auto: SevenSeas RM 420" with an edit icon. Ops can override by clicking to change the vendor. The idea is that routine orders should be one-click — the system makes the optimal choice.
Pros
- Fastest possible workflow for routine orders
- Optimizes cost by default — cheapest vendor always selected
- Reduces cognitive load on ops
- Could save significant cost over hundreds of orders
Cons
- Ops may not trust auto-selection initially
- Cheapest isn't always best (vendor capacity, relationship, reliability)
- Requires complete rate data for all vendors at all locations
- Vendor preferences exist for good reasons (customer requested, familiarity)

Recommendation: Option B — Vendor Comparison Popover

Why Option B: It gives ops the information they need to make a good decision without forcing a particular workflow. Power users who know their vendor just use the dropdown and never click "Compare." New ops or unusual routes benefit from seeing all options side by side. This respects tribal knowledge while making rate data accessible.

Why not Option C (auto-select): Auto-selection sounds efficient but requires two things we don't have yet: (1) complete rate data for all vendors at all locations, and (2) ops trust in the system's choices. If the auto-selected vendor is wrong (capacity issue, customer preference), ops has to override every time — which is slower than just picking from a dropdown. Build trust with Option B first, then consider auto-defaults later.

Implementation detail: The "Compare vendors" link only appears when 2+ vendors have rates for the same service + location. If only one vendor has a rate, no comparison needed. The popover shows: vendor name, rate, unit, estimated cost (rate × quantity), completion rate (last 90 days), and rejections (last 30 days). Sort by cheapest by default, with reliability as a secondary signal.
