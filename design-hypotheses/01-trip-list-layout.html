TripManager — Design Hypotheses

Three approaches to the trip list screen. Evaluating which best serves an ops planner scanning 50 trips/day.

Hypothesis A: Table-First

Inspired by Flexport + spreadsheets
Thesis: Ops planners are coming FROM spreadsheets. A table is the mental model they already have. Instead of fighting it, give them a better table — proper columns, inline job status chips, sortable headers. Progressive disclosure: click a row to expand full job details. This maximizes data density and scanning speed.

Risk: May feel too much like a spreadsheet and lack the "serious software" feel. Job cards don't fit naturally in a table.

Hypothesis B: Card-Grid (Refined Current)

Inspired by Linear + current design
Thesis: Trip rows expand to reveal parallel job cards. This is what we have now, polished. The key insight: jobs are parallel, and cards communicate that spatially. The collapsed row is compact for scanning; the expanded view gives full context.

Risk: Expanded state takes vertical space — only 2-3 trips visible when one is expanded. May feel slow for scanning many trips.

Hypothesis C: Kanban by Status

Inspired by Trello/Notion boards
Thesis: The ops planner's primary question is "what needs my attention?" A kanban board groups jobs by status (Pending / Active / Completed / Rejected) so they can see the pipeline at a glance. Each card is a job, not a trip. This inverts the hierarchy: jobs first, trips second.

Risk: Loses the trip-level view. Hard to see "how is trip X doing overall?" since its jobs are scattered across columns. May not match the mental model of managing trips.

Recommendation

Hypothesis A (Table-First) is the strongest for the primary use case. Here's why:

  • Scanning speed: Ops planners check 50+ trips/day. Tables are the fastest layout for scanning many rows. Each row shows customer, MAWB, route, and job status inline — no clicking to expand.
  • Mental model match: They're coming FROM spreadsheets. A better table is a lower cognitive switch cost than cards or kanban.
  • Data density: Job status chips inline (✓ Camila FM+OH) communicate more in less space than parallel card grids.
  • Exception surfacing: Rejected rows highlight with red background — visible at a glance without expanding.
  • Progressive disclosure still works: Click a row to expand the full job card view for detailed actions, status changes, proof upload, activity log.

Hypothesis B (card-grid) is the current design — it works but wastes vertical space when expanded. Hypothesis C (kanban) loses the trip-level grouping which is how ops think.

Proposed hybrid: Use Hypothesis A (table) as the default trip list view. When a row is clicked, expand to show Hypothesis B (job cards) inline. This gives maximum scanning density PLUS full job detail when needed.
