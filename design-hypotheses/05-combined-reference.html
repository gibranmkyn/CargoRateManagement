User-Facing Design Questions

Three interactions every ops planner will hit on day 1. Getting these right determines whether they stick with the tool or go back to spreadsheets.

1. Rejection & Reassignment

"How might we surface rejected jobs so ops can reassign the vendor within seconds — before the cargo sits idle?"
Real scenario: Camila's truck broke down en route to Huanggang Port. The job is rejected. 36 bags of priority AliExpress cargo are sitting at Qianhai Free Trade Zone with no one to move them. Every minute costs money and customer trust.

2. Action Feedback

"How might we give ops instant confidence that their action worked — without interrupting their flow?"
Real scenario: Ops just created a trip, changed 3 job statuses, and uploaded a proof document — all in 2 minutes. They need to know each action succeeded without stopping to check. Currently: silent redirect after creation, no feedback on status changes.

Design decision:

Toast notifications for all actions — top-right, auto-dismiss after 4 seconds. Green for success, amber for warnings, red for errors. Each toast includes: icon + action description + entity ID. Additionally: (1) newly created trips get a green left-border + "JUST CREATED" tag for 30 seconds, (2) status changes get a brief outline flash on the chip, (3) uploaded proofs show a "NEW" badge.

3. Editing After Creation

"How might we let ops fix mistakes in a trip without forcing them to delete and recreate it?"
Real scenario: Ops created a trip but selected the wrong customer. Or the MAWB was entered incorrectly. Or they need to add another job because the route changed. The trip has 2 jobs already in progress — deleting and recreating would lose all status history and activity logs.

Design decision:

Inline editing from the expanded trip view. Click an "Edit" button on the trip row → enters edit mode with a blue banner + Save/Cancel buttons. Rules: (1) Trip-level fields (customer, MAWB, bags, weight, remarks) are always editable until trip is validated. (2) Pending jobs can be edited or deleted. (3) In-progress or completed jobs are locked (shown with 🔒) — must cancel and recreate if changes needed. (4) New jobs can always be added. This matches the CR requirement: "only MAWB, bags, weight can be edited once trip is ongoing."
